AI as a Service

When we talk about implementing AI and ML services we refer to them as AI as a Service - or AIaaS -.

Before continuing, put yourself in the following scenario: You want to buy a coffee machine to make yourself a tasty, grinded coffee. You know you love coffee and how it tastes, but you have no idea about the machine, how it works. On top, you know nothing about coffee beans, their ideal grind size etc. Would you go straight away and buy the 15.000$ coffee machine? Obviously not.

The same happens with AI. In our vision, for the client to get the most out of it, he first needs to understand where it can be applied & how it works. In the next sections you will be able to understand how you can truly unlock AI's power tailored to your needs, through engineering.

But... What is AI?

Artificial Intelligence has been around for decades. Think about it: isn't it fascinating that a simple calculator‚Äîjust a small device with a few circuits, a display, and a battery‚Äîcan already outperform us in arithmetic? (If you're not convinced, try solving 9,84567/37 in your head without using a pen).

AI is a broad field that covers anything designed to mimic human reasoning: from basic hardware like calculators and computers to more advanced algorithms such as machine learning and deep learning (the engines behind systems like ChatGPT, Gemini, and others). You can visualize it like this:

But if AI has existed for so long, why has it only become a mainstream topic since 2022? The answer lies in data and computing power. AI achieved its first major breakthrough in the research community in 2017, when advances in computing and the explosion of available data allowed models to be trained on massive datasets using powerful clusters. Commercial applications followed just a few years later.

Now that we've defined what AI is, the real question is: how can you apply it to your needs? At Mirai, our engineers specialize in the two key branches of AI: Generative AI and Analytical AI.

Generative vs. Analytical Artificial Intelligence

Understanding the two fundamental approaches to artificial intelligence

Generative AI

Focuses on creating new content, from text and images to code and music. Its mission is to produce original outputs that are often indistinguishable from human creations. If you've heard of Agents, LLMs or used tools like ChatGPT, Gemini, etc., you've used Generative AI.

Analytical AI

A tool for finding patterns and insights within existing data. Its mission is to analyze, categorize, predict, and optimize. It helps us make sense of the past and present to better inform the future. You could use e.g. a model to predict customer churn based on his purchasing behaviour, or optimise for your next coffee been composition to produce the most aromatic and tasty coffee.
